On February 6th 2023

at 4:17 AM, the countries of Turkey and Syria were struck by a devastating magnitude 7.8 earthquake. About 9 hours later, an aftershock with a magnitude of 7.5 hit the same area. Throughout the next few days, these two countries and their cities of Gaziantep, Hatay, Kahramanmaras, Aleppo, and etc, were hit with hundreds of aftershocks.

The confirmed death toll is

41,000+

yet this number will continue to rise as rubble is cleared and bodies are found.

More than 25 million people have been affected by the earthquakes, including estimates of at least 7 million children.

However, not all hope is lost. Kerem Kinik, president of the Turkish Red Crescent, described the rescue and relief operations as a "marathon". He pointed out that relief operations in Turkey and Syria would continue for

Another 18 Months.
